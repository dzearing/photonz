Aurora Ridge — final cut.photonz

Every pixel above is composited by the same Metal renderer that ships in the app — zoom callout, annotations, and a non-destructively styled layer.

🎯

Zoom callouts

Drag a box over any detail (Z) and Photonz flies in a magnified callout with leader lines — rectangle or circle, adjustable magnification. The signature feature for docs and bug reports.

🖊️

Annotate anything

Arrows, rectangles, ellipses, highlights, and rich text with full font control. Drop them fast, then re-color, re-size, and drag endpoints anytime — annotations stay editable.

✂️

Crop, resize & transform

Crop with thirds grid and aspect locks, resize in pixels or percent with presets, set the canvas size with an anchor, and rotate or skew any layer.

🧅

Real layers

Promote any selection to a layer (⌘J). Add blur, opacity, borders, rounded corners and shadows — all non-destructive, applied live at render time.

📸

Capture & edit

Grab a region (⌘⇧4) or full screen (⌘⇧3) straight into Photonz, with a capture history carousel (⌘⇧H). Export PNG, JPEG, or HEIC at 1× or 2×.

⚡️

Native speed

Pure Swift and Metal-accelerated Core Image. A 12-megapixel, 10-layer document re-renders in well under one frame — interactive edits land in single-digit milliseconds.

First launch: Photonz is signed ad-hoc and not yet notarized, so macOS will warn you the first time you open it. Right-click the app and choose Open once — after that it launches normally.
